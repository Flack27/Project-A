
Classes lock in at 3 star and define a character's combat identity 
from that point forward. Every character falls into one of three 
archetype categories depending on their elemental awakening status.
Should steal things from other games into these classes.

---

## Physical Archetypes
The default path. Any character without an elemental awakening develops 
into a physical archetype. These cover standard combat roles — warrior, 
tank, assassin, support, debuffer and others to be defined. Which options 
appear at the 3 star choice is weighted by weapon techniques developed, 
basic passive rolled, and race affinity. Two or three options are presented 
at the moment of archetype lock, never a single forced choice.

Physical archetypes have no elemental expression in their active skills. 
Their element exists only as a matchup tag and a source for the minor 
passive at 4 star.

---

## Elemental Archetypes
Available only to characters who experienced an elemental awakening at 
2 star. Elemental awakening opens two paths unavailable to physical 
characters — mage and healer. Both use their awakened element actively 
in skills rather than just as a passive or matchup tag.

Mages deal elemental damage, their skill pool built around their specific 
element. A fire mage plays differently from a wind mage not just visually 
but mechanically — different status effects, different scaling, different 
team synergies.

Healers restore and protect allies through elemental affinity. True 
dedicated healing is only accessible through this path which makes 
healers naturally scarcer than physical archetypes. This scarcity is 
intentional — healers are worth investing in specifically because they 
cannot be replaced by just any character.

A character with an elemental awakening can still choose a physical 
archetype at 3 star if the options presented include one. The awakening 
opens elemental paths, it does not force them.

---

## Light & Dark Archetypes
Available only to characters with a light or dark elemental awakening, 
which is rare across all races with no strong affinity weighting toward 
any specific one. Every LD character is guaranteed a unique subclass 
at 3 star — every standard physical archetype has a named LD equivalent 
with a unique exclusive ability and access to LD specific passives that 
standard archetypes cannot roll.

LD subclasses are not stronger than their physical equivalents. They 
enable strategies and abilities that standard archetypes simply do not 
have access to, making them interesting rather than dominant. The rarity 
of LD characters comes from summon probability, not from mechanical 
superiority.

LD awakened characters who would naturally develop toward mage default 
to light mage or dark mage, elementally flavored versions of the 
standard mage with LD passive access on top.

Specific LD subclass names, their exclusive abilities and passive pools 
are a design feature for later once the standard archetype list is fully 
defined.

## PvP Archetypes & Supporting Mechanics

**Burst** — standard archetype, already covered by existing damage classes. SPD dependent, kills before the opponent can respond. Wind support is the natural enabler.

**CC / Attrition** — full debuff team that stacks more debuffs than the opponent can cleanse, slowly locking down action economy while dealing unavoidable damage. Four debuffers cycling effects. SPD relevant but not the primary win condition — effect accuracy and debuff variety matter more. Needs classes with broad debuff access and skills that apply multiple effects simultaneously.

**Bruiser** — high HP and DEF team that absorbs burst and wins through sustained damage. Countered by attrition teams with unhealable. Counters burst teams that can't one-shot through the bulk.

**Shield / Sustain** — team that front-loads mitigation to survive the opening burst window then outlasts the opponent. Key design requirement: at least one class that generates shields at combat start or on the first action regardless of SPD, making this archetype SPD-independent. Otherwise it collapses into a slower burst team. Loses to sustained DEF shred and unhealable stacking. Fits as a shell around burst or bruiser cores.

**Sacrifice** — class-specific direction rather than a full archetype. Classes or builds oriented around death and revival triggers — getting stronger as allies fall, reviving with enhanced states, treating ally death as a resource rather than a loss condition. Traits disabled in PvP so this lives entirely in class skills and passives. Needs at least one class with a meaningful on-death passive and one with a revive mechanic that changes combat state on activation rather than just restoring HP.

---

## Cross-Archetype Tools

**Resource denial** — bar pulling, silencing, disrupting action economy. Not a full archetype, fits into any team as a utility slot. A dedicated resource denial class would want bar pull on short cooldown, silence access, and possibly a passive that generates bar pull on debuff application. Slotting one denial unit into a burst or CC team changes how the opponent has to respond without changing the team's core gameplan.

---

## Combat Start Effects

Worth developing at least one class or subclass that generates a meaningful effect at combat start regardless of turn order — a shield, a barrier, a preemptive buff. This is the key unlock for making shield teams SPD-independent and creates a genuine counter to burst without requiring a SPD arms race. Could live on a specific guardian or earth-aligned class.

---

## Notes

Attrition and CC fold into the same archetype in practice — a pure debuff team is CC taken to its conclusion. The distinction is in execution not gameplan.

Sacrifice direction connects to existing trait design — last stand, survivor, pallbearer — but in PvP these are expressed through class skills and passives only since traits are disabled. Design these classes so the mechanic is self-contained in the kit.
