Every character belongs to a race from the shared world. Races coexisted before the stasis — they know each other, have history, which adds texture to team compositions without needing to explain inter-species dynamics every time. Race influences elemental awakening probability, weapon affinity, and carries a light racial identity that shows up somewhere in how the character plays. It is never the dominant factor in a character's build, just a consistent thread running through them.

---

## Races

### Human

The most common summon. No strong elemental affinity — humans distribute broadly across all elements with no meaningful weight toward any one. Weapon affinity is similarly open.

Racial identity: high variance. Human growth and aptitude rolls have a wider distribution than other races, producing more hidden gems and more duds. The most unpredictable summon and the most rewarding when a high roll surfaces.

### Elf

Affinity toward earth. Higher probability of earth elemental awakening than other elements. Natural tendency toward piercing weapons.

Racial identity: higher chance of gathering traits.

### Dwarf

Affinity toward fire. Higher probability of earth fire awakening. Natural tendency toward bludgeoning weapons.

Racial identity: higher chance of x traits.

### Beastman

Affinity toward wind. Higher probability of wind elemental awakening. Natural tendency toward slashing weapons.

Racial identity: natural weapons. Beastmen begin with claws as a default weapon rather than a crafted one. Claws are a slashing weapon that scales with the character rather than needing to be replaced through crafting — they grow as the character grows. Beastmen can still equip crafted weapons in place of claws if preferred. How claw scaling works mechanically is defined in the weapons system. See Loose Threads.

## Special Races

Available later in progression, tied to specific banners or events. Not part of the standard summon pool.

### Angel

Affinity toward light. High probability of light elemental awakening, near-certain but not guaranteed. Rare by design — linked to event banners that appear at specific points in the game rather than the standard summoning pool. Unlocks after tower floor 60.

### Demon

Affinity toward dark. High probability of dark elemental awakening, near-certain but not guaranteed. Same acquisition structure as angels — event banner tied, not standard pool. Unlocks after tower floor 60.

Angels and demons are not mechanically superior to standard races. Their value is the near-guaranteed LD awakening and the exclusive LD class paths that opens, combined with their rarity making them feel significant when they appear.

## Affinity & Tendency

**Elemental affinity** means a higher probability of awakening to that element at 2 star. It does not guarantee the awakening and does not prevent awakening to a different element — it just weights the roll. Characters of any race can awaken to any element, affinity just makes one outcome more likely.

**Weapon tendency** means faster technique development in the affinity weapon type. A dwarf picks up bludgeoning techniques noticeably faster than other weapon types. This feeds naturally into the archetype options that surface at 3 star — a dwarf who followed their weapon tendency will see tank and guardian options more prominently. Tendency is a nudge, not a lock.
