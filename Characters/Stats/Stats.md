Every character has four primary stats that define their combat capability, two hidden values that govern how they develop, and a set of secondary stats influenced primarily by gear and skills rather than the character themselves.

---

## Primary Stats

**ATK** — determines damage output on offensive skills and basic attacks. The primary scaling stat for warrior, assassin and mage archetypes.

**DEF** — reduces incoming damage. Primary scaling stat for tank archetypes. Some racial identities interact with DEF specifically — see [[Race]].

**HP** — total health pool. Loss of all HP removes a character from combat. Characters with higher HP can absorb more punishment before falling, making it valuable on frontline characters regardless of archetype.

**SPD** — determines how quickly the action bar fills and therefore how frequently a character acts. The most consequential stat in combat — small SPD differences compound over a full fight, fast characters can lap slow ones and act multiple times before they get a turn. See [[Combat]].

Primary stats have a base value determined by race and a variance range determined by growth rate. Two characters of the same race and star level will have similar but not identical stats — growth rate is the variable. High growth characters approach the top of their stat range at each level threshold. Low growth characters sit toward the bottom.

---

## Level Thresholds

Stats do not increase linearly with every level. Meaningful stat bumps happen at levels 20, 40 and 60 — the three thresholds within each star tier. Growth rate determines how generously these thresholds reward the character. A high growth character receives notably better stat increases at each threshold than a low growth one given identical investment.

This is where growth rate becomes most visible — two characters levelled side by side diverge meaningfully at threshold moments rather than gradually.

---

## Hidden Values

**Growth** — governs combat development. Affects XP gain rate, how generously stat thresholds reward the character, and how quickly weapon techniques develop through use. Visible in its effects over time but never shown as a number. Can increase through bonds, specific missions and significant moments. See [[Star Progression]].

**Aptitude** — governs trait development. Affects how quickly trait progress moves, how high each trait's personal ceiling sits, and how likely ascension rolls are to open a new trait slot. Independent from growth — a character can have high growth and low aptitude or vice versa. Can also increase through play. See [[Traits]].

Both values are seeded at summon but not fixed there. Discovering a character has grown beyond initial expectations is an intended experience.

---

## Secondary Stats

Crit rate, crit damage, effect accuracy and effect resistance exist as secondary stats. They are not meaningfully influenced by the character's base values — they are built through gear, runes and certain skills and passives. Characters begin with baseline values in each that are functionally equal across the roster. Differentiation here comes entirely from investment choices.

See [[Gear]] and [[Runes]].
