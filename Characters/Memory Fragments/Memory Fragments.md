Every character pulled from the stasis wakes up as an amnesiac. They know who they are in broad strokes — their name, their world, that time stopped — but the specifics of their life before are gone. Memory fragments are those specifics returning. Forgotten moments surfacing as a character grows.

Fragments are fully skippable. They are flavor for players who want it, invisible to those who don't. Nothing mechanically requires reading them. But for players who engage they build a picture of a real person who existed before the tower, which carries into how that character feels to play.

---

## Unlock Structure

**Fragment 1** unlocks at 1 star, triggered on summon. This is not a memory — it is a self-impression. The character in their own words describing who they are, as if asked directly. No past tense, no recollection, just what they know about themselves right now. In the tutorial this is framed as the Manager asking the question. After that it triggers automatically on summon and can be skipped or disabled entirely in settings.

**Fragments 2 and 3** unlock at 2 and 3 star respectively. These are memories — specific moments from the character's life before the stasis. Fixed and unchanging regardless of what the player does. The range is deliberately wide. A moment of peace, a loss, a choice, an ordinary Tuesday. Not every character's past is dramatic and that is fine. A quiet life rendered honestly is as affecting as a heroic one.

Higher star characters arrive with fragments already unlocked, reflecting that more of their memory has returned by the time they are summoned.

**Fragment 4** unlocks at 6 star. This is a reflection — who this character understands themselves to be now, after everything, in the lobby they have lived in and the tower they have climbed. Unlike the first three this fragment is shaped by the world the player has built — faction alignment, how the player has treated their characters, the moral choices made along the way. The same character can arrive at a different understanding of themselves depending on the Architect they serve.

This fragment is not fully defined until the faction and story systems are designed. See [[Story]] and Loose Threads.

## Traits & Seeding

Fragments are generated around a character's traits. If a trait is already unlocked when a fragment surfaces, the fragment provides context for it — the memory explains why that trait exists. If the trait is not yet unlocked, the fragment seeds it, either unlocking it immediately or creating strong momentum toward it.

A character whose first fragment presents them as someone who prefers their own company seeds lone wolf or guarded. A character whose second fragment is about losing someone to violence seeds vengeful or survivor. The text hints at it, a small trait tag on the fragment card shows the mechanical link — the trait name and whether it was seeded, nudged or unlocked by this moment.

Players who read fragments get a head start on understanding what a character can become. Players who skip develop traits organically. Neither is wrong. See [[Traits]].

## Presentation

Each fragment has its own format depending on type.

**Fragment 1** — first person, present tense. The character speaking as if asked who they are. 3 to 5 sentences. No title, no time anchor — just their voice.

**Fragments 2 and 3** — first person, past tense. A single moment, not a summary. Framed with a title and a vague time anchor. 3 to 5 sentences.

**Fragment 4** — first person, present tense. A reflection rather than a memory. Framed with a title. Tone varies based on the world the player built.

A trait tag sits alongside every fragment showing the mechanical connection — the trait name and whether it was seeded, nudged or unlocked by this moment.

_Example format — placeholder content:_

**Fragment 1** _I keep to myself mostly. Not because I dislike people — I just find I do better work when nobody is watching. Always have._ Trait: Lone Wolf — seeded

**"The Field"** — Before everything _I used to watch the grain move in the wind for hours. Nobody thought that was strange. Nobody was watching._ Trait: Calm — unlocked

## Normal Characters

Not every character has an interesting past and that is by design. A person who lived quietly, worked hard and loved ordinary things is a complete person. The fragments are not there to make every character feel like a protagonist. They are there to make them feel real. Mundane fragments done honestly are more affecting than forced drama, and they make the characters with genuinely extraordinary pasts stand out naturally.
