Every character has four active skill slots and five passives that unlock 
through star progression. Two active slots are weapon skills drawn from a 
learnable weapon tree, two are character skills tied to archetype. Weapon 
skills are fully player controlled and interchangeable. Character skills 
and all passives are permanently locked once learned.

Active skills define what a character does in combat. Passives define how 
well and in what specific way they do it. The two layers together with gear 
create the full character build.

---

## Active Skills

### Weapon Skills
Weapon skills fill the first two active slots. Rather than unlocking 
automatically at star thresholds, weapon skills are learned through a 
visible technique tree specific to each weapon type. Every weapon has 
4 to 6 learnable techniques visible from the start, allowing players 
to plan development direction before committing.

Development speed is tied to growth rate and time spent using that weapon 
in combat. A high growth rate character develops techniques noticeably 
faster than a low growth rate character using the same weapon. This makes 
growth rate feel meaningful in the early game before the stat threshold 
differences at level 20 kick in.

Techniques in the tree are fully interchangeable at any time for a small 
gold cost. These are trained skills not innate abilities — a character can 
retrain toward different techniques if content or team composition demands 
it. Switching weapons before archetype locks resets progress toward current 
techniques and begins building toward the new weapon's tree instead, no 
penalty beyond time investment redirected.

**Example sword tree:**
- Quick slash — fast low damage hit, high hit chance
- Heavy strike — slow high damage hit, chance to stun
- Defensive parry — reduces incoming damage for one turn
- Counter attack — triggers a free attack when this character is hit
- Wide slash — hits all enemies for reduced damage

**Example bow tree:**
- Aimed shot — single target, increased crit chance
- Rapid fire — two hits at reduced damage each
- Piercing arrow — hits through to a second target for partial damage
- Covering fire — reduces enemy SPD for one turn
- Pinning shot — chance to prevent target acting next turn

**Example hammer tree:**
- Crushing blow — high damage, chance to reduce DEF
- Ground slam — hits all enemies for reduced damage
- Shield bash — low damage, high chance to stun
- Fortify — increases own DEF for two turns
- Seismic strike — high damage, ignores a portion of enemy DEF

These are examples, not final lists. Each weapon type will have its own 
tree designed around the weapon's natural fighting style.

**Techniques developed before 3 star influence archetype options.** A 
sword user who heavily developed defensive techniques gets tank and guardian 
more prominently in their archetype choices at 3 star. One who went pure 
offensive gets warrior and assassin. Player decisions during the early game 
actively shape what the character can become rather than archetype direction 
being purely random.

The first two skill slots are always weapon tree techniques. Both slots are 
available from 1 star — a character starts with their basic weapon attack 
in slot one and develops their first technique to replace it as they train. 
Slot two opens at 2 star, giving the player a second technique slot to 
develop toward.

### Character Skills
Character skills fill the third and fourth active slots. They come from 
who the character is — their archetype, their memories, their identity — 
and cannot be changed under any circumstance.

**Slot three** unlocks at 3 star when archetype locks in. This skill 
directly expresses the archetype. A warrior gets an offensive cooldown 
skill, a tank gets a taunt or damage mitigation skill, a support gets 
a buff or heal, a debuffer gets a status effect skill. Awakened characters 
get an elementally flavored version rather than a generic one — a fire 
mage's first character skill hits differently to a generic warrior's even 
if they share a role.

**Slot four** unlocks at 4 star and deepens the archetype identity 
established at 3 star rather than introducing something new. A warrior's 
second character skill might be an execute threshold ability, a support's 
might be a group buff or cleanse, a debuffer's might be a multi-target 
status effect.

Five and six star add no new active skills. The kit is complete at 4 star. 
Late investment is about perfecting what exists not expanding the toolkit.

### The Archetype Dip
Through extensive training alongside characters of a different archetype 
a character has a small chance at 5 or 6 star investment level to absorb 
a single skill from outside their pool. A warrior who spent hundreds of 
battles alongside a mage might develop a basic elemental strike. A support 
who trained relentlessly with an assassin might develop a single target 
damage option. This is rare, emergent and tied to the training system 
rather than guaranteed progression. It sits outside the normal skill 
structure entirely and makes deeply invested characters feel like 
individuals who grew through experience.

## Passives

Five passives unlock through star progression, one at each star level 
from 2 to 6. All passives are permanently locked once unlocked. Passives 
draw from four pools — basic, archetype, element, weapon — with each 
star level drawing from a defined pool or combination. The alternating 
major and minor pattern creates a rhythm where big identity defining 
moments are separated by smaller refinements.

### 2 Star — Minor, Basic Pool
The character's first passive is a basic stat bonus drawn from a small 
pool of generic options. Simple and universal, works for every possible 
character regardless of what they eventually become. The passive rolled 
here acts as a soft signal toward compatible archetypes at 3 star — 
the player can read it and start making weapon technique and gear 
decisions to nudge their character in a direction before archetype locks.

### 3 Star — Major, Archetype Pool
The defining passive, unlocking at the same moment as the archetype. 
Directly expresses combat role and provides the scaling foundation the 
rest of the character builds around. Every archetype has a curated pool 
of major passives to draw from — the specific one rolled adds individuality 
within the archetype while always being coherent with the role.

### 4 Star — Minor, Element or Weapon Pool
A small passive drawn from either the element pool or weapon pool. 
Adds situational flavor tied to how the character fights and what 
element they carry. Does not compete with the archetype passive, 
just adds a layer of individual character on top of it.

### 5 Star — Major, Broad Pool
Draws from archetype, element and weapon pools combined. The most 
personal passive, reflecting the whole character rather than one 
aspect. Two characters of identical archetype and element can diverge 
meaningfully here. Drawn from a curated shortlist specific to the 
character's combination rather than fully randomly, ensuring coherence 
while still feeling like a discovery.

### 6 Star — Minor, Broad Pool
Final polish passive. Small refinement that completes the kit without 
adding new mechanics. The character is fully formed by this point, 
this passive makes them feel finished rather than introducing complexity.
