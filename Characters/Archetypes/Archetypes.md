
Archetypes lock in at 3 star and define a character's combat identity 
from that point forward. Every character falls into one of three 
archetype categories depending on their elemental awakening status.

---

## Physical Archetypes
The default path. Any character without an elemental awakening develops 
into a physical archetype. These cover standard combat roles — warrior, 
tank, assassin, support, debuffer and others to be defined. Which options 
appear at the 3 star choice is weighted by weapon techniques developed, 
basic passive rolled, and race affinity. Two or three options are presented 
at the moment of archetype lock, never a single forced choice.

Physical archetypes have no elemental expression in their active skills. 
Their element exists only as a matchup tag and a source for the minor 
passive at 4 star.

---

## Elemental Archetypes
Available only to characters who experienced an elemental awakening at 
2 star. Elemental awakening opens two paths unavailable to physical 
characters — mage and healer. Both use their awakened element actively 
in skills rather than just as a passive or matchup tag.

Mages deal elemental damage, their skill pool built around their specific 
element. A fire mage plays differently from a wind mage not just visually 
but mechanically — different status effects, different scaling, different 
team synergies.

Healers restore and protect allies through elemental affinity. True 
dedicated healing is only accessible through this path which makes 
healers naturally scarcer than physical archetypes. This scarcity is 
intentional — healers are worth investing in specifically because they 
cannot be replaced by just any character.

A character with an elemental awakening can still choose a physical 
archetype at 3 star if the options presented include one. The awakening 
opens elemental paths, it does not force them.

---

## Light & Dark Archetypes
Available only to characters with a light or dark elemental awakening, 
which is rare across all races with no strong affinity weighting toward 
any specific one. Every LD character is guaranteed a unique subclass 
at 3 star — every standard physical archetype has a named LD equivalent 
with a unique exclusive ability and access to LD specific passives that 
standard archetypes cannot roll.

LD subclasses are not stronger than their physical equivalents. They 
enable strategies and abilities that standard archetypes simply do not 
have access to, making them interesting rather than dominant. The rarity 
of LD characters comes from summon probability, not from mechanical 
superiority.

LD awakened characters who would naturally develop toward mage default 
to light mage or dark mage, elementally flavored versions of the 
standard mage with LD passive access on top.

Specific LD subclass names, their exclusive abilities and passive pools 
are a design feature for later once the standard archetype list is fully 
defined.
