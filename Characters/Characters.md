
Characters are the core of the game. Everything — the tower, the gear grind, the 
auto-battle system — exists to develop them. A character is defined by four things 
that build on each other: race, stats, archetype and skills. These unlock and 
develop over time rather than being fully visible from the start.

---

## Race

Race determines elemental affinity, stat leanings and default weapon tendencies. 
It never changes and is visible from the moment you summon a character. Races 
share a history in the same world so every combination in a team has context 
without needing explanation.

Current races and their natural leanings:
- Elves lean toward wind, piercing weapons, support and ranged archetypes
- Dwarves lean toward earth, bludgeoning weapons, tank and warrior archetypes  
- Beastmen lean toward fire, slashing weapons, aggressive combat archetypes
- Humans even spread towards all elements and weapons

Race determines the probability of elemental awakening at 2 star and influences 
which archetypes are more likely to emerge at 3 star. Nothing is guaranteed, 
just weighted.

---

## Elements & Weapon Types

Every character has both a weapon type and an element. For most characters these 
are linked. The element determines matchup advantages in both PvE and PvP.

**Weapon types and their default elements:**
- Slashing — Fire
- Bludgeoning — Earth  
- Piercing — Wind

**The trinity:**
- Fire beats Wind
- Wind beats Earth
- Earth beats Fire

**Light and Dark** sit outside the trinity entirely. 

Elemental matchup affects crit chance and effect accuracy modifiers in combat 
rather than raw damage — attacking into a weakness gives a crit bonus and 
improved effect landing chance, being attacked by a counter element gives the 
same penalty. Keeps matchup meaningful without making type-disadvantaged 
characters feel useless.

## Elemental Awakening

At 2 star there is a chance a character awakens with a genuine elemental affinity 
beyond their weapon type. This is not guaranteed and is weighted by race — an elf 
has a higher chance of wind awakening than a beastman. LD awakening has a low 
baseline chance regardless of race, making those characters rare across the board.

Awakening changes what archetype paths are available at 3 star. An unawakened 
character develops physical archetypes only. An awakened character gains access 
to elemental archetype paths — mage, elementalist, and other awakening-exclusive 
classes that use their element actively in skills rather than just as a matchup tag.

## Stats

Characters have four base stats: HP, ATK, DEF, SPD. All characters start with 
near-identical generic values at 1 star level 1. Small differentiation begins 
emerging at level thresholds — level 20, 40 and 60 — where hidden growth rate 
determines whether a character gets a small bonus to one or two stats. The 
variance is intentionally tiny, a few points at most, never enough to make a 
character feel broken or useless on base stats alone.

Speed is the most important stat in turn-based combat. Even small differences 
in SPD change turn order which can decide fights. Speed variance between 
characters is kept tightest of all four stats at base level — gear and passives 
do the heavy lifting for speed differentiation.

Gear is the primary driver of stat differentiation. Base stats set the floor, 
gear sets the ceiling. A character with low base SPD can compensate through 
gear, a character with naturally high base SPD needs fewer gear slots dedicated 
to speed leaving room for offensive or defensive substats.

Crit chance, crit damage, effect hit and effect resistance do not exist as base 
stats. They live exclusively in gear substats and archetype passives. Certain 
archetypes scale with them through passives — an assassin archetype might have 
a passive converting ATK into crit damage, a debuffer archetype might have a 
passive linking effect hit to SPD. This means these stats matter for specific 
builds without cluttering the base character sheet.

**Hidden growth rate** underlies all stat development. It determines how generously a character benefits at the level thresholds and how quickly weapon techniques develop. Hidden on purpose visible growth rate means instant culling of low rolls before attachment forms.

## Star Levels & Progression

Characters summon between 1 and 4 star. Five and six star are investment-only, 
never summoned directly. Each star level has a level cap before ascension 
is possible.

- 1 star: levels 1-10
- 2 star: levels 11-20
- 3 star: levels 21-30
- 4 star: levels 31-40
- 5 star: levels 41-50
- 6 star: levels 51-60

A summoned 4 star arrives with everything pre-generated through their tier — 
race, element, awakening status, archetype already locked, several skills 
already learned, both traits visible, multiple memory fragments already 
unlocked. They still require leveling through 31-40 before ascending to 5 star. 
The investment is real, you just skip the early discovery phase.

Star ascension triggers the key development moments. The ascension animation 
is the primary moment of reveal — traits unlock, memory fragments become 
available, stat threshold bonuses apply, and skill slots open. All of this 
is delivered in one satisfying moment rather than drip fed awkwardly.
