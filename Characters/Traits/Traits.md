Traits are things a character is or becomes through their life in the lobby and the tower. They are distinct from stats, class and skills — they define personality, capability and role in ways that bleed into combat behaviour, world activity and the texture of who a character is.

---

## Two Kinds of Trait

Traits cover two overlapping spaces without needing to be formally separated.

**Personality traits** describe how a character behaves: reckless, guarded, stubborn, calm. They feed into auto-battle tendencies, bond formation speed, and how a character responds to situations in the world.

**Vocation traits** describe what a character is good at outside of combat: blacksmith, researcher, leader, trainer, gatherer. They unlock activities in the lobby and on expeditions, and define what role a character plays in the broader world beyond fighting.

A trait is a trait. The distinction is for design reference, not something the player needs to learn.

Traits are split into flat and evolving types, with a third fused category that emerges from merging. See [[Flat Traits]], [[Evolving Traits]], [[Fused Traits]].

## Trait Slots

Every character has a hidden trait slot cap determined at summon and increasing at star ascension. Most characters cap at two. Occasionally three or four. Rarely one. The cap is never shown directly — players discover it by noticing a character keeps developing or has stopped.

Additional trait slots can be gained at star ascension. Each ascension rolls a chance to open a new slot, weighted by the character's aptitude score. A character raised from 1 star through to 6 star has had multiple chances to expand. A summoned 4 star has had those same rolls happen offscreen, arriving with slots already filled and traits already developed.

---

## How Traits Unlock

Traits are not unlocked by completing a checklist. They emerge from conditions being met repeatedly or meaningfully, combined with a hidden per-character aptitude for that specific trait.

Every trait has a hidden progress value per character. That value moves through relevant experience — the right activities, the right moments, the right company. Each character also has a hidden ceiling per trait. Some characters climb quickly toward a trait. Some never move because the aptitude is not there. Some start moving then stop before unlock.

A trait unlock is never guaranteed. Conditions make it possible. Aptitude determines how possible.

---

## Aptitude

Aptitude governs trait development specifically — how quickly a trait's hidden progress moves, how high each trait's personal ceiling sits, and how likely ascension rolls are to open a new slot. A high aptitude character accumulates traits more readily and tends toward a higher final slot count. A low aptitude character develops traits slowly if at all.

Aptitude is independent from growth rate. For full detail on both values see [[Stats]].

---

## Memory Fragments & Trait Seeding

Memory fragments do not directly unlock traits but they seed them. A fragment describing a character as a former academy instructor signals that the trainer vocation is reachable. A fragment showing them as someone who always fought alone signals a guarded or solo-leaning personality. Players who engage with fragments gain a head start on reading what to develop toward. Players who skip them develop traits organically through whatever activities they naturally use the character for.

Neither path is wrong. See [[Memory Fragments]].
