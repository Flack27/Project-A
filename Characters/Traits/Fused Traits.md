Some traits can merge into something neither alone could produce. The merged result carries the mechanical expression of both parents and represents a direction no single trait could reach. Merging frees a slot — two traits becoming one leaves room for something new to develop.

---

## How Merging Works

Merging uses the same underlying roll as evolution. Every trait on acquisition rolls whether it can participate in any of its possible merges — this happens once for flat traits at the moment they are acquired, and again at each development stage for evolving traits. If two traits that have both rolled into the same merge destination are present on the same character, the merge becomes possible.

It does not fire immediately. The character needs time and the right situations for both traits to be expressed together before it crystallises. Because both traits must independently roll toward the same destination, merges are genuinely rare. A character can have both leader and trainer for a long time and never produce mentor because one or both never rolled that direction. Another character produces the merge quickly because both rolled into it at acquisition. The player never knows which situation they are in until it happens or clearly does not.
