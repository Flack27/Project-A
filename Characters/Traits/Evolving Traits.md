Evolving traits describe a practice or a role. They have depth and develop through tiers — a novice blacksmith and a master blacksmith are meaningfully different. Each tier unlocks through relevant experience and then rolls whether a further tier exists for this character. Some cap at intermediate. Others reach master. The ceiling is hidden and set per character, influenced by aptitude. The interesting question is not whether a character progresses but how far they go.

Evolution within a trait is always opt-in when it fires. The player is notified that development is possible and chooses whether to allow it. A trait you built a strategy around will never change without your consent.

## Leader Variants

Core idea: fills a leader slot in a party, gives a passive party buff. Different base variants give different buffs, branches and fused traits build on top of these.

Base leaders:

- Vanguard — party ATK buff
- Tactician — party SPD buff
- Sentinel — party DEF buff
- Warden — party effect resistance buff
- Medic — party passive HP regen each turn
- Arcanist — party elemental damage buff

Possible branches:

- Aggressive — bigger offensive buff, party takes more damage
- Defensive — damage reduction, party deals less
- Specialist — buff only applies to specific classes or races in party
- Aura — buff extends to adjacent characters only, stronger than full party versions
- Sacrifice — leader takes damage on behalf of party in exchange for stronger buff

## Trainer

Core idea: accompanies another party, provides XP bonus, can prevent wipes if strong enough relative to that party.

- Trainer — basic XP buff to accompanied party
- Mentor — stronger XP buff, higher wipe prevention chance
- Drill Sergeant — lower XP buff but accompanied party gains bonus weapon technique development speed
- Coach — accompanied party gains bonus bond development speed
- Veteran — wipe prevention chance significantly higher, XP buff smaller, requires high investment level to unlock

## Blacksmith

Core idea: assigned to the smithy, improves output quality and variety beyond what building upgrades alone achieve.

- Apprentice — reduces material cost slightly
- Journeyman — occasionally produces higher quality gear
- Master — chance to produce rare gear unavailable otherwise
- Runesmith — (fused) specialises in gear with unique substats

---

## Researcher

Core idea: assigned to a research building, accelerates unlock speed and improves outputs.

- Student — accelerates lobby upgrade unlock speed
- Scholar — bonus detail on memory fragment unlocks
- Arcanist — occasionally produces unique research outputs
- Inventor — (fused) combines research with crafting outputs

---

## Alchemist

Core idea: produces consumables from expedition materials.

- Novice — produces basic consumables
- Adept — improved consumable quality and quantity
- Master — chance to produce rare consumables unavailable otherwise

---

## Cook

Core idea: assigned to the kitchen, improves food buffs for lobby characters.

- Home Cook — basic XP food buffs
- Chef — improved buff quality and duration
- Master Chef — unique buff types unavailable from basic cooking

---

## Gatherer

Core idea: improves resource yield and discovery on expeditions.

Base variants:

- Forager — bonus common material yield
- Surveyor — improved discovery rate of resource nodes
- Pathfinder — occasionally finds locations others cannot detect

Resource specific branches:

- Herbalist — bonus yield on plant and reagent materials
- Miner — bonus yield on ore and stone materials
- Hunter — bonus yield from beast type enemies on expeditions
- Scavenger — bonus yield from ruins and abandoned locations

Economy branches:

- Merchant — bonus gold from selling materials and gear
- Haggler — reduced cost on lobby upgrades and purchases
- Treasurer — bonus gold yield from all expedition sources
- Lucky Find — increased chance of rare material drops
- Hoarder — carries more materials per expedition without decay penalty
