
The tower is the spine of progression. Clearing floor milestones unlocks lobby upgrades, which raise the caps on character ascension, weapon tier, and rune quality. Everything else — rune farming, character levelling, weapon upgrading, building development — happens in the space between milestones.

| 6–15   | 2          | 2-star   | Tier 2      | Common (white)     |
| 16–30  | 3          | 3-star   | Tier 3      | Uncommon (green)   |
| 31–50  | 4          | 4-star   | Tier 4      | Rare (blue)        |
| 51–70  | 5          | 5-star   | Tier 5      | Legendary (yellow) |

Lobby tier 7 does not introduce a new rune tier. Instead it unlocks the artifact slot, a separate gear system with its own grind loop. See [[Gear]].

## Between Gates

Progress between milestones comes from several sources running in parallel.

Tower floors are repeatable and are the primary source of character XP. Farming the highest cleared floor is always the most efficient use of energy for climbing. Each floor range also unlocks progressively harder rune dungeon stages — five stages per dungeon, with drop rate increasing at each stage and rune advancement materials available at stage five.

Character levelling is driven by repeating floors, passive training ground assignment, and synthesis. Synthesis is the fastest burst source — a synthesized character contributes a fixed XP amount determined by their star level at the time of synthesis, compounding significantly at higher stars. Training ground is slow but runs passively. See [[Characters]]. Character ascension requires materials from trials and a rare resource dropped in the tower.

Weapons follow the same tier structure as characters. Tier upgrades require materials from dungeons and expeditions and unlock at the same lobby gates as the star ascension cap.

Character class skill upgrades require two materials: skill ascension materials and a rare drop. Skill ascension materials are farmed from the Trials — six dedicated dungeons unlocking at lobby tier 2. Each trial is element-specific; a character's elemental awakening determines which trial they draw from. Characters without an awakening use Trial of the Unbound regardless of weapon type. Every trial run produces skill ascension materials, with higher stages producing more per run. The rare drop is a separate material with a low chance of appearing from any content besides tower floors so dungeons, expeditions and trials. Its periodically boosted during events. Both materials are required to upgrade a skill.

Potions are produced at the laboratory from expedition materials. Characters with the alchemist vocation trait assigned to the laboratory improve output quality and variety. Potions are consumable combat items with a carry limit per battle.

## Progression Philosophy

Each gate should feel genuinely hard to reach. The bottlenecks are intentionally diverse — XP, ascension materials, rune quality, weapon tier, and skill upgrades all draw from different content and cannot be collapsed into a single farming activity. A player who focuses only on tower floors will be starved for rune advancement materials. A player who farms trials exclusively won't have the gold or rune quality to push through the next floor wall. Splitting attention across the full content loop is not optional, it is the design.

Once a gate is cleared the difficulty resets cleanly. Old tier content becomes faster to clear, resources that felt scarce become abundant, and catching secondary characters up to the new cap feels smooth. This is intentional — the reward for breaking through a gate is a period of genuine progression momentum before the next wall asserts itself.

Character levelling is an exception to this momentum. XP gain stays moderately slow throughout all tiers, even after a gate clears. Characters should never feel like they level trivially fast regardless of how efficient the farming is. The slow baseline is what makes synthesis feel meaningful and what keeps the roster management decisions real.

Characters are capped on the gear they can equip by their current star level. A 2-star character cannot equip Rare runes or Tier 3 weapons regardless of availability. This ensures that gear progression and character progression are always coupled — over-investing in gear for an underdeveloped character is not possible, and the incentive to ascend characters remains constant.
