Resources are the economic foundation of the game. Every system — summoning, building, crafting, progression — runs on one or more of these. The economy is designed so that spending decisions feel meaningful without ever feeling punishing. No resource should feel like a wall, just a choice.

---

#### Materials

**Expedition Materials** — 3 tiers each

- Wood — physical weapons, staffs, buildings
- Herbs — potions, bows, tomes
- Stone — buildings, glyph crafting
- Iron — physical weapons, weapon upgrades, buildings
- Crystal — orbs, glyph crafting, buildings
- Aether — magic weapons, potions, buildings

**Ascension Materials** — 3 tiers each

- Fire / Earth / Wind / Light / Dark / Unbound Sigils — skill upgrades / character ascension

**Rare Materials** — 3 tiers each

- Ascension Core — required for character ascension (tower low drop chance)
- Remnant — required for skill upgrades (expeditions, dungeons, trials low drop chance)
- Glyph Echo — glyph advancement (dungeons drop chance)

**Currencies**

- Gold — all economic actions
- Gems — summoning, energy recharge
- Scrolls — targeted summoning, element and race specific

---

#### Energy

Energy gates farm content and repeated plays. Tower floor first clears cost no energy — climbing is always free. Repeating floors for XP and materials costs energy. Dungeons cost energy per run. Expeditions are time gated rather than energy gated.

**Passive refill** Energy refills passively over time at a rate that supports comfortable daily play without requiring constant check ins. A player who logs in once or twice a day should have enough energy to feel productive without hitting a wall.

**Level up and milestone refills** Character level ups, star ascensions and tower floor first clears all grant energy bonuses. Early game these happen constantly making energy feel abundant during the tutorial and discovery phase. As progression slows the passive refill becomes the primary source naturally. This creates an organic early game where players can just play without hitting an energy wall before content variety opens up.

**Energy recharge** Crystals can be spent to recharge energy beyond the passive cap. Limited to a set number of recharges per day to prevent infinite farming through spending. The daily recharge limit means crystals spent on energy have a natural ceiling, preserving the summon versus energy tension without allowing pure pay to progress.

#### Summoning

Summoning uses crystals or scrolls. Pulls come in sets of five — single pulls are not available. Pulling in fives keeps the summoning experience feeling like an event rather than a single button press, without requiring the ten pull standard of most gacha games which would demand too many crystals per session given the economy.

**Crystal pulls** Draw from the full character pool. Rates weight toward lower star characters with higher stars possible but uncommon. No hard pity — instead a soft escalating rate increase that makes higher star characters progressively more likely the longer since your last one. Every crystal pull produces a usable character. Even a 1 star pull has value as a potential worker, a source of ascension materials through dismissal, or a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.

**Scroll pulls** Draw from a filtered pool based on scroll type — element or race specific. Shorter soft pity than crystal pulls reflecting the targeted nature of the spend. Used for intentional roster building rather than discovery. A wind scroll guarantees a wind character, removing the element of chance on the thing the player specifically needed while preserving surprise on which wind character appears and what their hidden potential looks like.

**Dismiss value** Every summoned character can be dismissed at any time, converting into ascension materials and XP for other characters. A character trained to 3 star before dismissal yields more than one dismissed immediately. This means even unwanted pulls have downstream value and the decision of when to dismiss versus continue investing is itself a meaningful resource management choice. Summoning to generate dismiss value is a valid strategy that naturally competes with spending crystals on energy.

**The core tension** Crystals spent on summons expand the roster and generate dismiss value but cost time to evaluate — a character needs to reach 3 star before their archetype and true potential are known. Crystals spent on energy accelerate development of known quantities. Neither is always correct. Early game summons feel exciting because everything is new and dismiss value is high relative to XP needs. Late game energy becomes more valuable as pushing established characters through 5 and 6 star requires significant farming. The tension shifts naturally with progression without requiring the economy to change.
