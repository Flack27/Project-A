Morale represents a character's current state of mind — how engaged, motivated and settled they feel in the lobby and in their role. It is distinct from growth and aptitude which represent hidden long term potential. Morale is visible, short term and responsive to circumstances.

Morale has a small influence on growth and aptitude. Not significantly — a character in a poor morale state develops slightly slower than their potential, a character in a good state approaches their ceiling slightly faster. The effect is never dramatic enough to make morale feel like a required management stat, just a gentle nudge in either direction.

---

#### Morale States

Morale is never shown as a precise number. It exists as one of five visible states displayed as an icon on the character portrait in the lobby. Hovering or tapping gives a vague flavour reason for the current state without exact values.

- **Thriving** — performing above their normal baseline
- **Settled** — the default healthy state, no effect
- **Restless** — something is off, vague tooltip hint at cause
- **Struggling** — visibly affected, development slightly slower
- **Broken** — significant cause, development notably slower, small combat performance penalty

Settled is the intended baseline. The system never requires active management to maintain Settled — default lobby assignment keeps most characters there naturally. Thriving is achievable through deliberate placement and relationship investment. Broken requires a significant cause and is not easily recovered from.

---

#### What Affects Morale

**Raises morale:**

- Winning fights and clearing content
- Living near or fighting alongside bonded characters
- Being assigned to a job matching a vocation trait
- Good food from a skilled cook in the kitchen
- Room quality relative to star level
- Certain traits that have a naturally higher baseline — calm, cheerful

**Lowers morale:**

- Losing fights repeatedly
- Death of a bonded character
- Being displaced from a higher floor for a lower star character
- Being benched for extended periods
- Certain traits with a naturally lower baseline — stubborn, prideful
- Trait conflicts in the lobby — specific trait combinations that create friction when characters are placed near each other

**Hard morale caps:** Some events place a hard ceiling on how high morale can climb regardless of placement or investment. A character whose closest bonded ally died in combat may be capped at Restless indefinitely. No amount of good food or nice rooms pushes them above that ceiling until the underlying cause is addressed or time passes.

How the player handles information flow to characters affects these caps. Whether a player tells characters the truth about what happened to lost allies, lies to them, or says nothing at all has downstream effects on morale caps. This system is not fully designed yet and belongs partly in the story document — noted here as an intended interaction.

---

#### Morale & Traits

Some traits interact directly with morale in the lobby context.

Traits can set a natural baseline — a calm character sits higher by default, a restless or reckless character sits lower. Traits can also create situational morale effects — a cowardly character assigned to dungeon content takes a morale hit, a stubborn character demoted in floor placement reacts more strongly than average.

Specific trait and morale interactions are defined as the trait list is built out.

---

#### Combat Effect

Morale has no combat effect at Thriving, Settled or Restless. A character at Struggling performs at their normal baseline — the penalty is purely on development speed. Only Broken carries a small combat performance penalty, reflecting that a character in genuine distress is not performing at their best.

The intent is that players who never engage with morale management stay at Settled through default systems and never see a combat penalty. The penalty only appears when something significant has gone wrong and the player has not addressed it.
