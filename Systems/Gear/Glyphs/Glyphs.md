Glyphs are equipped to characters to provide stats and set bonuses. Every character has six glyph slots. glyphs are freely swappable at any time with no binding on equip. The weapon occupies a separate slot and contributes to set bonuses but provides no glyph stats — see [[Weapons]].

---

## Tiers

Five tiers exist, identified by colour. Tier determines the value ranges a glyph can roll, the number of enhancement rolls it receives, and the likelihood of dropping with more substats already revealed. Higher tier glyphs are sourced from deeper dungeon content unlocked by lobby progression.

| Colour | Name | Enhancement Rolls |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| White | Common | 1 |
| Green | Uncommon | 2 |
| Blue | Rare | 3 |
| Yellow | Legendary | 4 |
| Red | Primal | 5 |

---
## Main Stats

Each glyph has one main stat determined by its slot. Three slots have fixed main stats — one each of ATK, HP, and DEF. The remaining three slots have variable main stats drawn from the following pools:

- Slot 4 — ATK, ATK%, HP, HP%, DEF, DEF% or SPD
- Slot 5 — ATK, ATK%, HP, HP%, DEF, DEF%, Crit Rate, or Crit Damage
- Slot 6 — ATK, ATK%, HP, HP%, DEF, DEF%, Effect Accuracy, or Effect Resistance

Main stats are determined on drop and can be rerolled at the smithy for gold.

---
## Substats

Every glyph has a main stat and up to four substat slots. On drop, a glyph reveals between one and four of its substats. The number revealed is random but weighted by tier a Primal glyph is more likely to drop with most or all substats showing, a Common glyph is likely to show only one or two. Any count from one to four is possible at any tier.

Substats not yet revealed are hidden until unlocked through enhancement rolls.

---

## Enhancement Rolls

Each tier grants a fixed number of enhancement rolls equal to its tier number. These rolls are applied at the smithy during enhancement. Each roll does one of two things: if fewer than four substats are revealed, the roll unlocks the next hidden substat. If all four substats are already revealed, the roll improves the value of a randomly selected existing substat within the glyph's tier value range.

A glyph's rolls are consumed during enhancement and do not reset. A fully enhanced Common glyph has used its one roll. A fully enhanced Primal has used all five.

---

## Tier Advancement

A glyph at maximum enhancement can be advanced to the next tier. Advancement carries the glyph forward — all previously applied rolls and their outcomes are preserved. The advanced glyph then has access to the one additional roll belonging to its new tier, resolved during enhancement at that tier. Value ranges on boosts and new substat rolls at the new tier reflect the new tier's ranges, not the original.

A Common glyph advanced through every tier to Primal accumulates five rolls total across its lifetime, identical in count to a native Primal. The difference is that earlier rolls resolved at lower value ranges. A Common glyph that dropped with four substats already revealed and advances cleanly can reach Primal with competitive stats — this is intentional, rare, and a function of drop luck rather than guaranteed outcome.

---

## Set Bonuses

Every set has a maximum of one instance per character — the same set cannot be equipped twice regardless of piece count. Weapon slots and glyph slots both contribute to set thresholds. The weapon's two set slots must belong to different sets.

**Ruin** 4/6 — Crit Damage +X%

**Defiance** 4 — Each time this character is hit by an attack, gain X% turn meter.
