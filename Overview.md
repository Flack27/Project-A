## Core Fantasy
Turn-based gacha RPG where every character matters because you raised them, not 
because they were rare. Chill background game you can have open with YouTube, 
as deep or as casual as you want. Long term progression that never really ends, 
story that never gets in your way but rewards you when you engage with it.

---

## The World
A world frozen in stasis — time just stopped, everyone in it suspended mid-moment. 
The characters you summon got pulled out before the freeze took them. They wake up 
in the lobby with no idea how they got there, just a feeling that climbing the tower 
is the way back. What caused the stasis, why these specific people got pulled, what 
the tower actually is — all unknown at the start and deliberately grey when it does 
unfold. No clean good vs evil, the truth is complicated and shaped by player choices 
along the way.

One shared world, all species coexisted there — elves, dwarves, beastmen etc. They 
know each other, have history, which adds texture without needing to explain 
inter-species first contact every time you build a team.

---

## The Lobby & Tower
Home base. Characters live here, walk around, interact. Expands as you progress, 
starts sparse and grows into something that feels inhabited. Tower rises from the 
centre.

Tower has no visible ceiling. Floors are a real challenge, you cannot blitz through 
them day one, clearing requires gear and team investment built over time. Big game 
modes unlock at milestone floors — dungeons, later guild content, later PvP. 
Discovered through climbing not handed over at the start. Floors repeatable for 
basic resources, dedicated gear grind lives in the dungeons branching off the main 
climb.

---

## The Architect & The Manager
Characters sense they are being guided by something, they call it the Architect. 
Not a god exactly, something older that built this space and set it in motion. They 
experience the player's actions as the Architect's will — gear changes, team 
decisions, all felt as an unseen hand. The player doesn't know if they actually are 
the Architect or just another piece of the mechanism.

The Manager is the in-between — a character who lives in the lobby, handles the 
tutorial, and knows more than they let on. They give just enough explanation to get 
characters moving without resolving anything. Has seen something like this before 
maybe. Won't say.

## Characters
AI generated — unique card art per character, 3D model assembled from modular 
presets based on the card. Card and model correspond visually without needing true 
AI 3D generation. All species from the shared world so every character has a place 
in the lore.

No character is objectively better than another. A 1 star raised well can match a 
4 star. Higher stars start with more developed skills and better base stats but the 
ceiling is accessible from anywhere with investment.

Characters have a hidden growth rate that determines how fast they develop. Hidden 
on purpose — visible growth rate means instant culling of low rolls which kills 
attachment. Growth rate can increase through bonding with other characters or 
completing specific missions. Occasionally you discover you've been raising a hidden 
gem which feels great.

As characters advance they recover memories — fragments of who they were and what 
their life was before the stasis. Fully skippable but there if you want it. Adds 
personality context that carries into combat behavior and auto-battle tendencies.


---

## Auto-Battle & AI
Not just use most expensive skill on cooldown. Auto-battle intelligence scales with 
how trained a character is. Characters trained together learn each other's patterns 
and start playing around each other — a support learns to hold a cleanse for when 
the specific debuffer on the enemy team acts rather than burning it immediately. 
Solo trained characters play selfishly, team trained characters enable combos.

Long term this could go into actual reinforcement learning territory — characters 
literally developing optimal play through simulated battles together. Fits the 
world flavor that these characters are growing and learning, not just stat sticks.


---

## Story & Choices
Light touch. Flavor text per floor, character memory fragments as you advance 
them, the Manager drip feeding context. Never blocking, always skippable, 
rewarding if you engage.

Two major branch points as the story unfolds, each splitting the player 
community into factions with minor mechanical flavor — small passive buffs that 
fit the philosophical choice made. First choice creates 2 factions, later choice 
splits both again into 4 total. Neither path is clearly correct, the grey is 
the point. Drives community discussion and makes your world feel like yours.
