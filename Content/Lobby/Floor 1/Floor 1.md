
Floor 1 is the base. Available from the start, never locked behind progression. Every building here is functional from day one and upgradeable over time. New building types do not unlock on floor 1 — depth comes from upgrading what exists rather than expanding the building list.

---

#### Summoning Gate

Where characters enter the world. The primary point of engagement with the summon system. Crystal pulls and scroll pulls both happen here. Always accessible regardless of lobby progression.

The gate is central and visible from most of the lobby. Characters arriving through it is a visual moment — they appear, disoriented, meeting the space for the first time.

---

#### Dorms

Where characters sleep and live when not assigned elsewhere. Floor 1 dorms are basic — small rooms, shared spaces, functional but sparse. Houses the full roster at the start since no upper floors exist yet.

Room size and quality are upgradeable. Larger rooms reduce capacity, smaller rooms increase it. Quality upgrades improve resident morale. Floor 1 dorms at maximum upgrade are still notably worse than default upper floor dorms — the prestige gap between floors is intentional and meaningful.

As upper floors unlock higher level characters migrate upward by default. Floor 1 dorms become home to newly summoned and lowest investment characters over time.

Upgrade path — room size, room quality, total capacity. Costs basic craft materials available from early expeditions and tower floors.

---

#### Storage

Holds materials, gear and resources. Starts with a base capacity that fills quickly without upgrades. Overflow materials are lost so storage upgrades are an early priority for most players.

Purely functional. No assignment, no morale interaction, no vocation tie in. Just space.

Upgrade path — capacity only. Cheap and straightforward, upgraded frequently in early game.

---

#### Smithy

Gear crafting. Takes materials from expeditions, tower floors and dungeons and produces weapons and equipment. Basic smithy produces common gear and occasionally uncommon. Higher upgrade levels unlock better recipes and improve baseline output quality.

A character with a blacksmith vocation trait assigned here improves output quality and speed beyond what building upgrades alone can achieve. An unassigned smithy operates at baseline. Higher blacksmith tiers produce results the building alone cannot replicate regardless of upgrade level — the vocation matters as much as the building.

Upgrade path — output quality, crafting speed, recipe variety. Mid tier upgrades require materials from dungeon content.

---

#### Expeditions

Where gathering parties are dispatched. Characters are assigned to an expedition, given a destination and a duration, and return with materials after the time passes. Expedition slots are limited — the player cannot run infinite simultaneous expeditions, creating a soft resource on how much passive gathering runs at once.

Active gathering is available as an alternative — a more hands on version where the player participates directly for faster returns. Passive expeditions run in the background while the player does other things. Both produce the same material types, active is faster, passive requires less attention.

Characters with gatherer vocation traits return better results. Higher gatherer tiers unlock expedition destinations unavailable to unspecialised characters.

Upgrade path — expedition slots, expedition duration reduction, destination variety. Unlocks deeper destination types as the tower progresses.

---

#### Sanctum

Handles character recovery and synthesis. Two functions in one building, both dealing with the state of characters between activity and inactivity.

**Recovery** — characters who were defeated in combat recover here over time. Recovery speed is baseline at first, upgradeable. A defeated character is not available for combat until recovered. The recovery time is a real cost that makes character loss in combat feel meaningful without being permanent.

**Synthesis** — characters can be synthesized here, converting them into experience and ascension materials for other characters. This is a significant and irreversible action. The building does not editorialize about what synthesis means in the world's logic — that is left deliberately ambiguous and is a story concern addressed elsewhere.

The name Sanctum is intentionally neutral. What happens here to characters narratively is one of the grey moral questions the game leaves unresolved.

Upgrade path — recovery speed, synthesis yield. Neither upgrade changes the nature of what the building does, just the efficiency.

---

#### Design Notes

Floor 1 buildings are intentionally limited in type. The depth of the lobby comes from upgrading and populating these buildings over time, not from adding new ones on the same floor. New building types unlock on floors 2 and above as tower progress opens them.

All floor 1 upgrades use materials available through normal early play. Nothing requires content locked behind floor 10. Players can fully develop floor 1 on resources gathered in the natural course of early progression.
