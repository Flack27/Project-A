
Handles character recovery and synthesis. Two functions in one building, both dealing with the state of characters between activity and inactivity.

**Recovery** — characters who were defeated in combat recover here over time. Recovery speed is baseline at first, upgradeable. A defeated character is not available for combat until recovered. The recovery time is a real cost that makes character loss in combat feel meaningful without being permanent. Will be very long for higher characters, costs money to reduce.

**Synthesis** — characters can be synthesized here, converting them into experience and ascension materials for other characters. This is a significant and irreversible action. The building does not editorialize about what synthesis means in the world's logic — that is left deliberately ambiguous and is a story concern addressed elsewhere.

The name Sanctum is intentionally neutral. What happens here to characters narratively is one of the grey moral questions the game leaves unresolved.

Upgrade path — recovery speed, synthesis yield. Neither upgrade changes the nature of what the building does, just the efficiency.
