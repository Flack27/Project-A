The lobby is the player's home base. Characters live here between fights, buildings provide services that support progression, and the tower rises from the centre. It starts sparse and grows into something that feels genuinely inhabited as the player climbs.

The lobby is not a management game. Default systems handle assignment and operation without player input. Depth exists for players who want it but nothing requires active management to function.

---

#### Structure

The lobby expands vertically as the player progresses through the tower. New floors unlock at tower milestones, each bringing new buildings, increased capacity and deeper systems. The tower itself is always visible from the lobby, central and impossible to ignore.

Characters are assigned to floors by default based on their current level. Higher floors have better facilities and nicer dorms. Manual reassignment is always available for players who want control over placement.

#### Characters in the Lobby

Characters are not static between fights. They exist in the lobby visibly — walking around, working at buildings, interacting with each other. The lobby feeling alive is a priority even at its most basic. Specific character behaviors, animations and interactions are a technical and art direction concern developed separately.

Characters have jobs assigned to buildings, sleep in dorms, and train passively. These activities drive passive development between active play sessions. Details of what each building does and how job assignment works are defined in the individual floor documents.
