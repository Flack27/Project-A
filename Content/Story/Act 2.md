## The Lobby Consensus

The characters return to the lobby. They know the worlds are gone, dismissal isn't death, and the architect was guessing in arc 1 the same way everyone else was. There is no fresh choice for the architect here. The lobby has metabolized the truth and the manager surfaces the consensus, speaking for the characters who lived through it.

The shape of the consensus is determined by the arc 1 choice. The arc 1 buff locks in and upgrades into a stronger version with a new name reflecting where the lobby has landed.

### Pessimistic arc 1 path → **Resolve**

The manager:

"They thought they were going to die when you dismissed them. They were braced for it. Then they found out they're already past that — whatever death was, it happened when the world ended, and they've been on the other side of it the whole time. They're not afraid of dying anymore. They can't be, it's already done. What they want now is to be alive again in a way that counts, and if the throne does what the angels and demons said it does, you're the one who can give them that. So they're climbing. Not because they're scared. Because there's something on the other side worth reaching for."

The pessimism buff upgrades into Resolve. Same direction, stronger effect. The lobby has accepted the weight and chosen the climb on the other side of it.

### Optimistic arc 1 path → **Reach**

The manager:

"They believed you when you said they'd go home. They held onto that the whole climb. Now they know there's no home, and they're not pretending otherwise. But they're also not done. You're still here, the tower is still here, and souls go somewhere even if nobody knows where. They'd rather be moving toward something than sitting with nothing. If the throne lets you find out where they go, that's closer to home than staying still. So they're climbing. Not because they think you'll fix it. Because you might."

The optimism buff upgrades into Reach. Same direction, stronger effect. The hope has reshaped from return to forward motion.

### Neutral arc 1 path → **Steady**

The manager:

"You never told them what would happen. You said you didn't know, and you were right not to pretend otherwise. They noticed. They've been climbing without a promise this whole time, and now the truth is out and it doesn't break anything for them because nothing was built on a lie. They know what the climb is for now. They're climbing. The same way they have been, just with the answer in their hands."

The neutral buff upgrades into Steady. Same direction, stronger effect. The lobby's composure is vindicated and carries forward.

## Manager and Character Tone Shifts

The locked consensus changes how the lobby sounds across arc 2. The manager and characters speak slightly differently depending on which path locked. This is dialogue tuning, not new systems.

**Resolve lobby.** The manager is harder, more direct, less softening. She doesn't dress things up. Characters are sparing with words, focused, the lobby has a weight to it that wasn't there before. Bonds form around shared steadiness rather than warmth. Combat barks lean toward determination and finality.

**Reach lobby.** The manager is gentler but more honest, the warmth from arc 1 carries forward without the evasions. Characters are open, talkative, the lobby feels populated and lived-in. Bonds form around hope reshaped into mutual support. Combat barks lean toward encouragement and forward motion.

**Steady lobby.** The manager is calm, measured, slightly more candid than before. The composure she held in arc 1 settles into something closer to honesty. Characters are even-keeled, the lobby reads as functional and clear-eyed. Bonds form around mutual respect. Combat barks lean toward focus and presence.

These shifts apply across all manager dialogue, character idle barks, combat lines, and floor reveal text from floor 51 onward. The lobby has a tone now and the tone reflects what the lobby decided about itself.
