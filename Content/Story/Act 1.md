Arc 1 covers from the architect's emergence through the floor 50 boss fight and the truth drop. It ends the moment the boss falls and the reveal lands.

## Setup

The architect emerges in the lobby. No physical form, no history, no memory of before. Conscious enough to govern and choose. The manager is already there, waiting, genuinely glad the architect arrived because she can't climb without one. Her helpfulness is real but self-interested. She knows the lobby and the tower. She doesn't know much beyond that, or claims not to.

The lobby existed before the architect. The tower rises from it. Nobody has reached the top.

Characters begin being summoned. They wake amnesiac, knowing their name and their world in broad strokes, knowing time stopped, not knowing the specifics of who they were. They are told to climb the tower for answers. The architect is the one telling them.

## The First Choice

Early in arc 1 the manager asks what to tell the characters what happens when one of them is synthesized or dismissed. The architect has three options:

Optimistic — tell them they go back to their world. A lie. There is no world to go back to.

Pessimistic — tell them they die. A lie. Dismissal is not death, souls go somewhere else.

Neutral — tell them you don't know. The honest answer. Souls do go somewhere and the architect doesn't know where.

Characters remember what they were told. Alignment shifts based on the choice. Switching frequently triggers a credibility penalty. Neutral functions as a hold state, no alignment shift, no commitment.

Optimistic gives a boost to Aptitude and Raises the Morale ceiling
Pessimistic gives a boost to Growth and Raises the Morale floor
Neutral gives a smaller boost to both and no Morale ceiling or floor increase
## The Climb

Arc 1 climbing covers floors 1 through 50. Systems unlock floor by floor. The lobby grows. Characters develop, bond, accumulate memory fragments.

Guilds unlock during this arc. Explained simply as other architects and lobbies you can work with. The fact that other architects exist is presented as ordinary, the manager is matter-of-fact about it.

The climb is framed throughout arc 1 as the way to get answers about the synthesized and about the characters' worlds. The architect is presented as the one in control, the manager as guide, the tower as a path forward.

## The Angel/Demon War

Around floor 40 angels and demons appear in the tower. They are at war with each other inside the tower. The climb forces the architect through their conflict. Both sides recognize the architect as an architect and ask for help. Both promise information in exchange. The architect chooses a side. The unchosen side becomes the opposition for floors 40 through 50. The chosen side fights alongside or provides support, exact form to be defined.

## The Floor 50 Boss

The floor 50 boss is the leader of the unchosen faction. Big angel if the architect sided with demons, big demon if the architect sided with angels. Defeating it ends the war arc.

## The Truth

The chosen faction delivers on their promise after the boss falls. The truth has three parts:

The worlds the characters came from already ended. They are not in stasis waiting to return, the worlds are gone.

What the architect has been summoning and dismissing is souls. Not living people, not characters who die when dismissed. Souls returning to wherever souls go, which neither faction claims to fully know.

The top of the tower has a throne. Claiming it makes the architect a god, or something close enough that the distinction doesn't matter.

The reveal recontextualizes everything in arc 1. The optimistic lie about returning is exposed. The pessimistic lie about death is exposed. The neutral hold is vindicated as honest, though it didn't help anyone either. The climb continues because the throne is now the visible prize.

## The Reward

The chosen faction grants the architect the right to summon angels and demons as part of the lobby roster. Souls of the chosen type, not the warring beings encountered in the tower. One is granted for free immediately as part of the floor 50 payoff.

The off-faction summon pool also opens. Angels and demons can be summoned regardless of which side the architect picked. These are souls, not the faction beings, and they have no loyalty to the tower factions. The war up there is not their war.

## Arc 1 End

Arc 1 ends the moment the floor 50 boss falls and the truth lands. Characters return to the lobby changed. The locked-in choice that opens arc 2 follows immediately.
