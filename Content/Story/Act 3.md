Arc 3 is the epilogue. It covers the floor 100 boss fight, claiming the throne, and everything after. Deliberately undefined at this stage. By the time players reach floor 100 the design context will be deep enough to choose between several directions, and locking one in now constrains the next several years of development.

## The Floor 100 Boss

The throne is occupied. The floor 100 boss sits on it. The fight is the final climb encounter. Defeating it is the gate to claiming the throne.

What the boss actually is, why it sits there, what defeating it means for the world and the architect, all open. Candidate framings to be developed when arc 3 design begins:

A previous architect who reached the top and was bound by the throne rather than freed by it.

Something the tower itself produced as a final test or final defense.

A god that already exists, that the throne belongs to, that the architect must replace.

The accumulated weight of every soul that has been dismissed, given form by the tower.

The manager, or what the manager has been all along.

None of these are committed. The boss is a fixture in the structure, its identity is a later design decision.

## Claiming the Throne

The first lobby to defeat the boss claims the throne. Claiming makes the architect a god, or something functionally equivalent. The synthesis question is fully answered at this point — where souls go, what dismissal does, whether the architect can reach them.

Whether claiming is permanent, whether the throne can be refused, whether it binds the claimer or frees them, all open.

Could become a seasonal thing, climb to the tower top first one to reach it gets rewards and is the boss for the next tower climb? or decides what happens and based on that next season changes.

## Post-Throne Directions

Three candidate directions for what happens after the throne is claimed. Not mutually exclusive. Final direction to be chosen when arc 3 design begins.

**More tower.** Reaching the throne reveals more structure above, below, or parallel to the tower. New climb, escalated stakes, same loop. Standard live-game move, works if the reveal lands.

**Cycle.** Claiming triggers a reset. The tower regrows or reshuffles. Previous progress becomes foundational rather than lost. The experienced architects that exist elsewhere are previous winners. Explains why the system persists, gives infinite content runway.

**Threshold.** Claiming moves the architect into a different mode of play while the tower continues for others. Post-tower content gated by the throne. The climb keeps running for new architects and for those still ascending.

## Open Threads

The unresolved questions that arc 3 either answers or deliberately leaves open:

What the synthesized characters' fate actually is, and whether the throne lets the architect reach them.

What's at the top of the tower beyond the throne and its occupant.

What caused the worlds to collapse or freeze.

The manager's full nature and history.

What the floor 100 boss is.

What happens after the throne is claimed.

Whether the synthesized are recoverable and how that interacts with the throne.

What the manager's stake in the climb actually is.

Whether the architect is a role being filled or an entity with its own history.

What the tower actually is at its deepest level.

These are not problems to solve before arc 1 and arc 2 ship. They are the design space arc 3 will occupy when it gets developed.
