Three things drive every design decision in this game. When something feels off 
it's usually because one of these is being violated.

---

## Attachment Over Optimization

Every system in this game is designed to make you care about your characters 
rather than evaluate them. Hidden growth rates exist so you never cull a low roll 
before giving it a chance. Locked in skills exist so no character is just a 
rerolled version of the meta. Characters can be dismissed freely at any time, converting into experience for other characters. The attachment the game creates should come from the systems themselves, not from making it hard to let go. If a player wants to feed a character they raised for fifty hours into a new favorite that should feel like a meaningful choice not a forced one. Personality carrying into combat exists so two characters with identical stats still feel different.

The moment a player can fully optimize their roster from a spreadsheet the 
attachment dies. The goal is that two players with the same characters have 
completely different experiences with them based on how they raised them.

---

## Depth Over Content Volume

The systems should be interesting enough that a small roster played for a long 
time beats a huge roster of disposable units. Synergy training, archetype 
discovery, skill interactions, auto-battle intelligence that grows with investment 
— all of this means the game gets deeper the longer you play rather than requiring 
a constant stream of new content to stay fresh. New floors and new characters add 
to the game but the core loop should never feel stale without them.

---

## F2P As The Default Experience

Spending money should never feel required and a player who never spends should 
feel like a full citizen of the game. The monetization is built around two ideas — 
keep the ceiling low, and never sell power directly.

The pull system has two tracks. Gold pulls are always accessible through normal 
play, giving everyone a constant low-level engagement with the gacha loop. Rates 
for higher rarity characters are lower here but never zero — the excitement is 
always alive. Premium pulls use summoning scrolls which are rarer, earned slowly 
through play and through events, and represent the bigger moments. Both tracks 
give access to the same roster, premium just arrives there faster.

On top of this a battle pass will exist as the main monetization layer, 
deliberately designed to be long running to kill weekly FOMO pressure. Limited 
direct purchase options for scrolls may exist but need careful balancing — 
the line between convenience and pressure is easy to cross and the intent is 
firmly on the convenience side.

The specifics of rates, scroll economy and battle pass structure are not set in 
stone yet and need playtesting to get right. The principle however is fixed — 
spending accelerates, never dominates.

---

## Where These Create Tension

These three principles pull against each other sometimes and that is worth 
acknowledging. Deep systems can hurt accessibility for casual players. Attachment 
design means protecting bad decisions a player made early which can frustrate 
progression. F2P friendliness can make the gacha feel less exciting if pull 
currency flows too freely. None of these tensions have a clean answer, they get 
managed case by case, but the philosophy above is the tiebreaker when a design 
decision is unclear.
